Hydraulic lifts are able to move cars upward when a pump in their structure pushes a liquid from a fluid container to a hollow cylinder. As the cylinder fills with the liquid, a piston component inside it is forced to move upward, which generates the movement of the lift. The initial movement of the pump is activated with electricity.
